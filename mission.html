Our Mission

Why we built this tool — and what we hope it changes.

Meetings cost more than you think

The average professional attends over 25 hours of meetings per week. Most organizations track headcount, software subscriptions, and office expenses to the dollar — but rarely stop to ask how much a standing weekly meeting actually costs in salary alone.

The answer is often surprising. A one-hour meeting with eight mid-level employees can cost several hundred dollars. Multiply that by 52 weeks, and a single recurring meeting can consume tens of thousands of dollars per year that most teams never consciously account for.

Clarity, not cuts

We are not anti-meeting. Collaboration is essential, and some of the most valuable work happens in a room together. Our goal is simply to give teams the information they need to be intentional about how they spend that time.

When you can see the cost of a meeting laid out clearly — broken down by person, by recurrence, by year — you ask better questions. Does everyone in this meeting need to be here? Could this be an async update instead? Could we cut it from 60 minutes to 30?

Private by design

All data you enter into this tool — names, salaries, meeting details — stays entirely on your device. Nothing is sent to a server, stored in a database, or shared with anyone. There are no user accounts and no tracking of any kind.

Disclaimer

While your data never leaves your browser, it is worth knowing that browser extensions installed on your device may be able to access information stored locally. We recommend only using this tool on a personal or trusted device.
